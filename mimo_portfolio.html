What is this project

Overview of the MIMO Antenna Design

This project presents a compact four-port dual-band MIMO antenna for WLAN and ISM band applications. Using an octagonal patch geometry with CPW feeding on an FR-4 substrate, the antenna achieves excellent impedance matching, low mutual coupling, and strong diversity performance — making it suitable for next-generation wireless systems including 5G, defence, and IoT.

📡

CPW Feeding

Coplanar waveguide technique simplifies fabrication and improves impedance matching across both frequency bands.

🔷

Octagonal Geometry

Innovative octagonal patch with concentric ring creates dual-band resonance through multiple current path lengths.

🔗

Low Mutual Coupling

Optimized element placement achieves ECC < 0.05 and diversity gain ≈ 10 dB without complex decoupling networks.

📶

Dual-Band Operation

Simultaneously covers 2.48 GHz ISM and 5.11 GHz WLAN bands with S11 below −10 dB at both resonances.

Background & Context

Introduction to MIMO Antenna Technology

The increasing demand for high data rates, reliable wireless links, and efficient spectrum utilization has led to the widespread adoption of multiple-input multiple-output (MIMO) technology. MIMO systems significantly enhance channel capacity and link robustness by exploiting spatial diversity without requiring additional bandwidth or transmit power.

📱

Wi-Fi & IoT

MIMO antennas are fundamental in modern Wi-Fi (802.11ac/ax), IoT networks, and short-range communication systems.

🛡️

Defence Applications

Compact dual-band operation at ISM frequencies enables secure communication in tactical and military scenarios.

📶

5G Integration

Multi-port MIMO configurations are foundational to 5G massive MIMO base stations and user equipment.

🔬

Research Motivation

Achieving compact size, dual-band, and low ECC simultaneously on low-cost FR-4 is the core challenge addressed.

Design Challenges

Key Challenges in Compact MIMO Design

Mutual Coupling

Close proximity of multiple antenna elements leads to strong mutual coupling, degrading impedance matching and radiation efficiency. High mutual coupling increases ECC and reduces diversity gain.

Dual-Band Resonance

Achieving simultaneous resonance at 2.48 GHz and 5.11 GHz within a compact footprint requires careful patch geometry modification and current path engineering.

Low-Cost Substrate

Using FR-4 (εr = 4.4) introduces higher dielectric losses compared to PTFE-based substrates, requiring optimized design to maintain acceptable radiation efficiency.

MIMO Performance Metrics

Simultaneously achieving ECC < 0.5, DG ≈ 10 dB, and TARC < −10 dB across both bands is a multi-objective optimization challenge.

Key Techniques in Literature

Isolation & Decoupling Methods

⚡

Neutralization Lines

Connecting adjacent antenna elements via a transmission line to cancel mutual coupling. Demonstrated effective for USB dongle and WLAN module applications.

🌐

Defected Ground Structure

Ground plane modifications that suppress surface currents and reduce mutual coupling without increasing antenna size or complexity.

🔋

Electromagnetic Bandgap

EBG structures placed between antenna elements to block surface wave propagation and enhance both isolation and radiation efficiency.

🧲

Parasitic Elements

Additional passive elements near the antenna to decouple neighbouring ports through destructive interference of coupled electromagnetic fields.

Research Gap

What This Work Addresses

Despite extensive research, few designs simultaneously achieve:

  • 1

    Wide −10 dB impedance bandwidth at both 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz on a single-layer FR-4 substrate without multilayer complexity.

  • 2

    Comprehensive parametric study linking antenna geometry, feed configuration, and ground-plane modifications to dual-band resonance and isolation.

  • 3

    High isolation (20 dB) and low ECC with a simple octagonal CPW-fed structure avoiding complex decoupling networks.

  • 4

    Stable dual-band operation across all four ports with consistent MIMO performance metrics (ECC, DG, TARC).

Project Goals

Objectives of This Research

The proposed work aims to design, simulate, fabricate, and characterize a compact four-port dual-band CPW-fed MIMO antenna for modern wireless communication applications, addressing the limitations of existing designs.

  • 01

    Dual-Band Resonance: Design a compact antenna simultaneously resonating at 2.48 GHz (ISM/WLAN) and 5.11 GHz (WLAN/5G) with S11 below −10 dB at both frequencies.

  • 02

    Four-Port MIMO Configuration: Extend single-port dual-band design to a four-port MIMO configuration with inter-element spacing of approximately λ/2 for optimal diversity.

  • 03

    Compact Footprint: Achieve overall antenna dimensions of 62 mm × 62 mm on a single-layer FR-4 substrate (εr = 4.4, thickness 1.6 mm) without multilayer or multi-board approaches.

  • 04

    Low Mutual Coupling: Minimize port-to-port mutual coupling to achieve ECC < 0.5 and maintain high port isolation (>20 dB) across both operating bands.

  • 05

    CPW Feeding Technique: Implement coplanar waveguide feeding to simplify fabrication, improve impedance matching, and avoid via-holes associated with conventional microstrip lines.

  • 06

    Octagonal Patch Geometry: Utilize octagonal patch with concentric ring to achieve dual-band operation through multiple current path lengths without external filters.

  • 07

    MIMO Performance Metrics: Validate diversity gain (DG ≈ 10 dB), envelope correlation coefficient (ECC < 0.05), and total active reflection coefficient (TARC < −10 dB).

  • 08

    Practical Applications: Ensure suitability for real-world deployment in Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, IoT, 5G sub-6GHz, and defence wireless communication systems.

Radiation Characteristics

Radiation Pattern Analysis

🔴

2.48 GHz Pattern

Well-defined main lobe with semi-directional radiation. Strong surface currents along outer octagonal ring edges confirm fundamental resonant mode excitation. Stable gain suitable for Wi-Fi/IoT.

🟠

5.11 GHz Pattern

Quasi-omnidirectional radiation with strong gain indicated by red/orange regions. Smooth main lobes with no deep nulls ensure stable signal strength for 5 GHz WLAN applications.

🟡

Current Distribution

At 2.48 GHz: outer edge concentration. At 5.11 GHz: localized inner edge currents. Adjacent port currents remain low, confirming high isolation across both bands.

🟢

TARC Performance

Pronounced minimum near 5.11 GHz with TARC dropping below −7 dB, confirming efficient power coupling under active excitation of all ports simultaneously.

Roadmap

Future Scope & Extensions

The current design establishes a solid foundation for future enhancements. Multiple avenues exist to improve performance, extend functionality, and adapt the antenna for emerging wireless standards and applications.

📡

5G mmWave Extension

Scale the octagonal MIMO geometry to operate in the 28 GHz and 39 GHz millimetre-wave bands for 5G NR applications, maintaining compact form factor.

🌐

8-Port MIMO Array

Extend to an 8×8 MIMO configuration for massive MIMO base station applications supporting Wi-Fi 6E and 5G cellular infrastructure.

🧪

PTFE Substrate

Replace FR-4 with Rogers RT/duroid (εr = 2.2) to reduce dielectric losses and improve radiation efficiency, particularly at the 5.11 GHz band.

🔧

Triple-Band Operation

Incorporate a third resonance at 2.4 GHz / 5.8 GHz / 60 GHz through additional concentric ring elements or slot perturbations in the ground plane.

🛡️

Defence FHSS Integration

Integrate frequency-hopping spread spectrum (FHSS) circuitry for tactical military communication systems requiring jam resistance and covert operation.

🤖

AI-Assisted Optimization

Apply machine learning (neural networks, genetic algorithms) to automatically optimize patch dimensions and feed positions for specified MIMO metrics.

🔬

On-Body Testing

Evaluate antenna SAR (Specific Absorption Rate) and detuning effects for wearable IoT and body-area network applications.

📱

Smartphone Integration

Miniaturize and adapt the design for integration within smartphone chassis, managing hand effects and proximity to metallic structures.

✈️

Aeronautical UAV

Ruggedize the design for UAV/drone platforms requiring wide-temperature-range operation and vibration resistance at dual ISM bands.

Summary

Conclusion

This work successfully presents a compact four-port dual-band MIMO antenna operating at 2.48 GHz and 5.11 GHz on a single-layer FR-4 substrate, meeting all design objectives and demonstrating practical suitability for modern wireless systems.

The proposed antenna achieves wide −10 dB impedance bandwidths at both operating frequencies, high port-to-port isolation of 20 dB, low envelope correlation coefficient, and enhanced diversity gain — ensuring robust MIMO performance suitable for multi-user wireless systems including Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, 5G, IoT, and defence applications.

Key Findings

Summary of Contributions

  • ✓

    Novel Octagonal CPW Design: The octagonal patch with concentric ring and CPW feeding achieves dual-band resonance through shape-based current path engineering, avoiding external filters and maintaining single-layer simplicity.

  • ✓

    Excellent Impedance Matching: S11 values of −38.9 dB at 2.48 GHz and −25 dB at 5.11 GHz — both far exceeding the −10 dB design threshold — confirm excellent impedance matching and minimal reflected power.

  • ✓

    Strong MIMO Metrics: ECC < 0.5 for all port combinations, DG ≈ 10 dB (near-ideal), and high port isolation of 20 dB collectively validate excellent diversity performance suitable for MIMO communication.

  • ✓

    Fabrication Simplicity: Single-layer FR-4 construction with 35 μm copper metallization and CPW feeding eliminates via-holes and multilayer complexity, making the design cost-effective and reproducible.

  • ✓

    Measurement Validation: Close agreement between HFSS simulation and VNA measurement confirms the accuracy of the design methodology and the quality of fabrication.

  • ✓

    Broad Applicability: The combination of dual-band, four-port MIMO, compact size, and low-cost substrate makes this design directly applicable to commercial Wi-Fi, 5G, defence, and IoT products.
